#	Flexible Electricity Tariffs and Consumer Behavior
Dynamic Pricing  •  Demand Response  •  Energy Modeling  •  Consumer Behavior

This project examines the impact of flexible electricity tariffs on consumer behavior and future energy grid investment. Using choice experiments and surveys, it assesses price sensitivity and willingness to shift energy consumption in response to dynamic pricing. These insights are integrated into energy system modeling to evaluate effects on grid load, peak demand reduction, and investment needs for expansion and storage.  The findings will help design tariff structures that balance economic efficiency, consumer acceptance, and energy system stability.

## Research Questions
*	How price-sensitive are residential consumers when facing dynamic electricity tariffs?
*	To what extent can demand shifting through flexible tariffs reduce peak grid load in the modeled system?
*	What tariff structures best balance consumer acceptance with grid stability and investment efficiency?
